Dress Code

Dancers are expected to wear proper attire and hairstyle to all of their classes. Neat appearance and grooming make for a disciplined dancer, and more importantly, shows respect and cooperation.

All Classes: Hair should be pulled away from face and off the neck.

Street clothes are not permitted to wear during any class.

Dance shoes used inside the studio should not be worn outside.

NO JEWELRY is to be worn during class.

Dancers will receive one warning if proper attire and footwear is not worn. After that, they will be asked to sit out and observe.

Jeté's Tinys/Minis/Babycakes classes: Any style or color leotard and tights. Skirts/Tutus allowed if above the knee. Ballet/Tap shoes required. Boys may wear any comfortable dance attire such as shorts, leggings, a snug fitted singlet or t-shirt. Convertible tights are recommended for tumbling classes.

Ballet: Class attire should include a neatly secured bun, form fitting leotard (black or pink preferred), and clean, non-ripped tights (tan or pink). Ballet shoes are required and should be clean and properly fitted. Skirts may be worn as long as they are above the knee. Warm up wear (i.e. shrugs, leg warmers and wraps) may be worn for the first 10 minutes of class.

Hip-Hop: Comfortable clothing. No jeans, no skirts, no boots, no flip flops, no elevated shoes of any sort. Sneakers mandatory. Baggy clothing acceptable. Must still be dance or active wear attire, no street clothes.

Jazz/Contemporary/Lyrical/Tap/Tumbling: Any style leotard or dance/athletic attire such as leggings, shorts, sports bras, tight fitted t-shirts. No clothing may be worn to obstruct the view of the body (i.e. baggy shirts/pants). Proper shoes required.
